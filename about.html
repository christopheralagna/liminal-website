ABOUT LIMINAL

WHAT WE ARE

Liminal is an anti-capitalist arts and culture center and community collective. We are volunteer-run and consistently open to the public as a safe space for queer and marginalized folx to work, hang out, rest, get access to free material resources, hold meetings for community organizing initiatives, and temporarily escape the threat of police violence and individual harrasment which exists every day on the streets (especially within the SouthSide neighborhood where we are located).



We provide free access to clothes, art supplies, stationery items and working materials, radical books and zines, a computer and printing station, an all-gender accessible public restroom, and PWYC meals and drinks. We host space for local art exhibitions, radical film and webinar screenings, safety trainings, queer celebrations, group and panel discussions, political education, skill-sharing sessions, clothing exchanges, food gatherings and initiatives, mutual aid, performances, and other sorts of events and community programs.

OUR PURPOSE

The word ‘Liminal’ implies an in-between space: somewhere within the margins of what is easily defined or categorized.



Our core motivation is to create collective spaces for exploring liberation and fulfillment through diverse modes of community involvement, while centering queer people and marginalized folx.



We believe that the mergence of grassroots activism/education and queer space/arts/culture/celebration is a praxis which can motivate genuine community care, solidarity, and action.
