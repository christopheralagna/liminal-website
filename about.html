ABOUT LIMINAL

THE NAME

Liminal is unlike common iterations of public spaces - the name itself implies existing within the margins of what is easily defined or categorized.

WHAT WE OFFER

Liminal provides a free open work-space, free art supplies, anti-capitalist education, community skill-sharing, mutual aid networking, and many other programs/events!

When we say 'anti-capitalist', we understand this to imply the work of anti-racism, anti-imperialism, and decolonization.

We also strive to host private meetings and public events of other local radical organizations for free. Contact us to request use of Liminal for your group.

OUR PURPOSE

Our core motivation is to create collective spaces for exploring liberation and fulfillment through new modes of community/artistic involvement, and the centering of marginalized and queer people/topics.

THEORY + PRAXIS

Liminal can be understood to occupy both a physical space and a mental/emotional arena: a space to come in contact with intrinsic value in our lives, untangled from capitalist modes of value. In the act of coming together in order to reconsider new ways of living/being, we hope to make a significant impact on our collective cultural consciousness- so that we can each become empowered as active agents in matters of community + resistance (both on the local level and beyond).

Hours of Operation

Tuesday + Thursday, 1pm - 5pm (We hope to be open daily in the near future)

During these hours, we are open to the public as a LGBTQIA+BIPOC safe-space for working // hanging-out // and making art!

We provide snacks and drinks from our bar on a suggested-donation basis, although it is not necessary to purchase or consume anything. Outside food/drink are welcome! BYO anything (but no alcohol please)!

We are also open during the times of scheduled classes and events

Our Chosen Location

We find it critical to discuss the physical location of our space as it relates to the geopolitical conditions of our area- to do so allows us collectively to understand why this location is endangered and how to resist forces of gentrification.

Liminal is located in so-called Flagstaff: formally colonized as a city in 1876. We are spatially located in the Southside Neighborhood, which rests between the two largest local industries: downtown tourism (north) and Northern Arizona University (south). These capitalistic forces are primary perpetrators of Flagstaff gentrification. The Southside Neighborhood - historically home to many low-income, unsheltered and BIPOC (black, indigenous, and people of color) folks - is being socially, economically and culturally encroached upon. As a result, many people are becoming victims of displacement, state-sanctioned violence and intimidation.

Liminal intends to challenge the forces of Flagstaff gentrification through raising awareness of local issues, teaching political education, organizing community action and giving space to other radical organizations.

Nonprofit Information

Liminal is a non-profit corporation and shall be operated exclusively for charitable and educational purposes within the meaning of section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code.

In accordance with these requirements, Liminal intends to meet the particular goals set forth below:

  • To support the inclusion of low-income, queer, and otherwise underprivileged members in our community, by providing a free everyday working space and cafe that hosts art exhibitions, educational workshops, and other special events to bring people together.
  • To encourage a deeper local appreciation of and collective involvement with art and culture, especially for the marginalized and underprivileged members of our community, by providing free materials, offering free educational resources, and by organizing artist meetups.
  • To better unify our community by showcasing local art and educating about cultural diversity, through the active representation of individuals facing the effects of institutionalized oppression.
  • To promote continuing education and community collaboration by helping organize workshops that employ people without professional certification, so that they can both share and better value their individual skillsets.
