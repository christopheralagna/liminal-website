ABOUT LIMINAL

WHAT WE ARE

Liminal is a nonprofit café and community center that is completely volunteer-run. All purchases are PWYC (pay what you can) and you never have to buy anything in order to spend time here. Furthermore, café sales and donations go directly into keeping the space up and running.

We have many free community resources and amenities which include clothing, art supplies, stationery items and working materials, harm reduction kits, radical books and zines, a public computer and free printing, an all-gender accessible public restroom, and a small classroom.

We frequently host local art exhibitions, film and webinar screenings, safety trainings, queer-friendly parties, community meetings, classes and workshops, resource exchanges, free food gatherings, community fundraisers, performances, and other sorts of programs and events.

We are open from 10am-6pm everyday.

OUR PURPOSE

The word ‘Liminal’ implies an in-between space: somewhere within the margins of what is easily defined or categorized.

Our intention is to explore diverse and intersecting forms of communal activity- to create ‘liminal’ spaces that are in opposition to oppressive binaries which underlie disconnected and hierarchical relationships.

We believe that an intersectional anti-capitalist framework centered in arts, culture, education, celebration, food, and other means of connectivity, can motivate genuine community care, involvement, and fulfillment.
