ABOUT LIMINAL

THE NAME

Liminal is unlike common iterations of public spaces - the name itself implies existing within the margins of what is easily defined / categorized. At a glance the space looks like the mergence of a cafe // exhibition space // community education center.

WHAT WE OFFER

Liminal will provide a free open work-space, free art supplies, anti-capitalist classes, co-working opportunities, community meetings, workshops, panels, artist-matching, exhibitions, food initiatives, mutual-aid networking, and act as a marketplace for locally made goods.

THE MISSION

Liminal’s core motivation is to create collective spaces for reimagining liberation and fulfillment through new modes of community action, artistic intervention, and the centering of marginalized people/topics.

THEORY + PRAXIS

Liminal can be understood to occupy both a physical space and a mental/emotional arena: a space to come in contact with intrinsic value in our lives, untangled from capitalist modes of value. Meanwhile, the process of coming together to reconsider new modes of living can be understood as an evolving piece of art in itself: an installation that can bring about long-term cultural reprogramming.

More Details

Liminal is a non-profit corporation and shall be operated exclusively for charitable and educational purposes within the meaning of section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code.

In accordance with these requirements, Liminal intends to meet the particular goals set forth below:

  • To support the inclusion of low-income and otherwise underprivileged members in our community, by providing a free everyday working space and cafe that hosts art exhibitions, educational workshops, and other special events to bring people together.
  • To encourage a deeper local appreciation of and collective involvement with art and culture, especially for the underprivileged members of our community, by providing free materials, offering free educational resources, and by organizing artist meetups.
  • To better unify our community by showcasing local art and educating about cultural diversity, through the active representation of individuals facing the effects of institutionalized oppression.
  • To promote continuing education and community collaboration by helping organize workshops that employ people without professional certification, so that they can both share and better value their individual skillsets.
