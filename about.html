ABOUT LIMINAL

THE NAME

Liminal is unlike common iterations of public spaces - the name itself implies existing within the margins of what is easily defined/categorized. At a glance the space looks like the mergence of a cafe // exhibition space // community advocacy center.

WHAT WE ARE

Liminal can be understood as a community center rooted in the arts and social/communal justice, with the opportunity for community to address issues, solidarity, aid, networking, and integration.

WHAT WE OFFER

Liminal will provide open work-space, anti-capitalist classes, co-working platforms, community meetings, workshops, panels, artist-matching, exhibitions, food initiatives, mutual-aid networking, and more.

THE MISSION

Liminalâ€™s core motive is to offer these modalities of action, artistic intervention and centering of marginalized people/topics in order to offer collective spaces for reimagining liberation and fulfillment.

THEORY + PRAXIS

Liminal can be understood to occupy both a physical space and a mental/emotional arena: a space to come in contact with intrinsic value in our lives- untangled from capitalist modes of value. Meanwhile, the act of bringing our community together & offering an alternative mode of living can be understood as a piece of art in itself: an installation meant to bring about long-term cultural reprogramming.
